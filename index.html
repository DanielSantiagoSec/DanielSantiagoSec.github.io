01 · About

Ten years in the operational trenches makes the framework real.

I spent over a decade inside the operations engine of a Fortune 5 company — running logistics, leading incident triage for enterprise "war rooms," and hardening access controls across 500+ operational roles, long before I had a formal title for it. Somewhere in there I realized I wasn't just running operations, I was securing them.

The frameworks I now study — NIST CSF, Zero Trust, least privilege — aren't theory to me. I spent ten years living the operational chaos they exist to prevent.

That realization turned into a B.S. in Cybersecurity — 3.91 GPA, Summa Cum Laude, Data Analytics concentration — a CompTIA Security+ certification, and a growing stack of hands-on IAM and cloud security work. I'm channeling a decade of operational discipline into identity management, access governance, and Zero Trust design.

Outside of work, I'm a new dad, and most of my "why" these days comes from watching my daughter experience things for the first time. When I'm not deep in a security rabbit hole, you'll find me chasing new towns, new food, and new experiences — and I know that is true because my wife has occasionally instituted the occasional "no more cyber security this week" rule, so I'm either doing something right by the people in my network, or I'm becoming a little obsessed. I co-founded Lehigh Valley Rent-a-Chef, used to compete with a powerlifting group through a gym called Iron Core Fitness, snowboard, and ride motorcycle — and I spent time doing equine therapy with my own horse, Maverick, working with clients facing neurological deficiencies.
